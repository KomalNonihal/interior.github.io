Recent Projects

Modern

Modern interior design is described by the sleek, uncluttered, and less busy style that began in the late 19th century. Modernism reflects a fuss-free approach to interior designing. Patterns are much less to be seen.

Mid-century

Mid-century interior design style marks an artistic era that spanned from the 1930s to the 1960s. It also forms a chunk of the ultimate aesthetic cleanse, modern design but with some slight differences in this style

Contemporary

As the name suggests, contemporary designs reflect the present-day scenario. We can also call it current and trending. This interior design style is all about the here and now. Therefore, it's very dynamic by nature.

Traditional

By traditional style, we mean literally "Traditional" where interiors are unmatchable. It represents the grandeur of the 18th and 19th centuries. This style takes you to the time, where symmetrical detailing and an aesthetic sense of art areof utmost importance.

Eclectic

This one is the best considering it allows freedom of choice. In short, eclectic style is a mix of different styles. It’s important to note that there are not many hard and fast rules when it comes to this style other than the overall look shouldn’t fit into any category.

Rustic

Rustic design is a style that's simply natural, aged, organic, unpretentious, and sometimes even a little distressed. It shows the skin textures of different materials truthfully which is the beauty of it. It can be sub-categorized as sub-themes like coastal,Tuscan
